Quality Metric
Customer Delivery Feedback

CDF tracks customer-reported feedback on their delivery experience. It focuses on controllable behaviors that affect satisfaction and trust, such as following instructions, securing packages, and maintaining professionalism.

When customers receive their package, they can rate the delivery as "Very Good" or "Not Very Good." If "Not Very Good" is selected, they choose a specific reason. These DA-controllable reasons become the basis of CDF. Examples include wrong address, ignored delivery notes, unprofessional behavior, or unsafe package placement. The goal is to identify coachable behaviors that impact customer experience.

Common Feedback Causes
  • Package left in unsafe, visible, or weather-exposed area
  • Delivered to wrong address or wrong unit
  • Customer instructions not followed or ignored
  • Driver appeared unprofessional (attire, behavior, or loud music)
  • Package mishandled (thrown, dropped, or placed carelessly)
  • Photo did not match the drop location
Driver Success Tips
  • Always double-check name, address, and unit before scanning
  • Follow customer delivery notes exactly. If unsafe or unclear, call the customer or Driver Support
  • Choose secure, concealed spots protected from view and weather
  • Ensure your uniform, music, and conduct reflect professionalism
  • Take a clear POD photo showing placement and surroundings
  • Respect property. Avoid walking on lawns or landscaping
  • If unsure, contact Driver Support. Never guess a location
Quality Metric
Delivery Completion Rate

DCR measures the rate at which packages are successfully delivered on the first attempt versus returned to station (RTS). It tracks driver behaviors that contribute to failed delivery attempts and helps identify coaching opportunities to improve first-attempt delivery success.

Package-Level Proactive DCR Exemptions

Drivers are eligible for package-level DCR exemptions for RTS packages when the corresponding exemption requirements are met.

Contact Compliance
Applicable RTS Codes
Unable to Access (UTA) · Unable to Locate (UTL) · No Safe Location (NSL) · Unsafe Due to Dog (UDD) · Customer Unavailable · Bad Weather
Requirements
DA must complete ONE of the following:

Option 1: Contact Customer. Call customer and allow phone to ring for at least 12 seconds (2 ring cycles). If customer answers, ask for help completing delivery. If no answer, send a text message.

Option 2: Contact Driver Support. Call Driver Support if customer number is disconnected, unlisted, or if DA does not feel safe contacting customer. Between 8 PM and 8 AM local time, DA must contact Driver Support only.
Business Closed
Applicable RTS Codes
Business Closed
Requirements
Automatic exemption when ONE of the following is met: package was routed outside of known business hours; no known business hours associated with the location; or business was unexpectedly closed (driver arrived during business hours and remained within 50m for 30+ seconds).
Reprocessed at Station
Applicable RTS Codes
Out of Delivery Time (OODT) · No RTS Code Selected · Object Missing
Requirements
Automatic exemption when station reroutes packages while driver is on route. Valid when we confirm the driver is not in possession of the package.
Object Missing
Applicable RTS Codes
Object Missing
Requirements
Automatic exemption when we validate that 1) package was marked "Object Missing" by DA, and 2) later delivered by another DA without returning to station.
Amazon Locker
Applicable RTS Codes
Locker Space Insufficient · No Locker Available
Requirements
Automatic exemption when DA encounters no space in Amazon locker.

Applicable RTS Codes
OTP Not Available

Automatic exemption when customer is unable to provide correct OTP.
Outside Driver Control
Applicable RTS Codes

Automatic exemptions for package issues beyond DA control.
Keys to Success
  1. Rely on your Amazon delivery app when attempting to access apartments or multi-unit buildings. Access codes will be provided in the app. Make sure to attempt all access codes given to you.
  2. Contact the customer or Driver Support if facing difficulties delivering. A valid contact enables a proactive exemption for six DCR RTS codes: UTL, UTA, NSL, UDD, Customer Unavailable, and Bad Weather.
  3. Find secure places to deliver packages that are not visible from the street, have little foot traffic, and are protected from weather. If you cannot find a safe location, leave a "we missed you" card near the door.
  4. Trust your delivery route. It may seem circular, but it is built to minimize U-turns, left turns, and other inefficient maneuvers.
  5. Do not skip stops on the route. Skipping a stop increases the chance that when you return, the business will be closed.
  6. Follow standards to minimize missing packages. Ensure you physically have all packages when leaving the station, do not condense containers or combine packages into separate bags, and scan each package if rescuing to validate a virtual/physical match.
  7. Download offline maps on your delivery device for your area before starting your route.
  8. Damaged packages should not be delivered. If the QR code cannot be scanned to RTS, read the tracking number and type it in.
  9. Take a clear photo of delivery (POD) for all applicable deliveries, including surroundings, to help the customer locate the package.
Quality Metric
Delivery Success Behaviors

DSB measures instances where customers reported "Delivered Not Received" (DNR) and the issue was linked to a DA-controllable behavior. It ensures packages are safely and securely delivered, reducing the chance of DNR concessions.

DSB monitors the most common controllable delivery errors that cause customers to report missing packages. It focuses on five key behaviors: Simultaneous Deliveries, Delivered more than 50m from Geopin, Incorrect Scan Usage (Attended or Unattended), and No Photo on Delivery. By preventing these, drivers help protect customer trust and avoid avoidable defects.

Common DSB Causes
  • Package delivered more than 50 meters from the Geopin location
  • Incorrect scan type used (e.g., Delivered to Customer vs Unattended)
  • No POD taken when required
  • Multiple packages delivered simultaneously to different addresses
  • Delivered to a public or highly visible area instead of a secure spot
Driver Success Tips
  • Verify address, unit number, and Geopin before delivery
  • Deliver within 50m of the Geopin in a secure location
  • Always take a POD photo when prompted. Retakes are fine
  • Use correct scan codes that accurately reflect the delivery type
  • Never place packages in mailboxes (federal violation)
  • Follow customer instructions if safe to do so; otherwise call Driver Support
  • Check labels carefully in group stops to avoid mixing addresses
Keys to Success
  1. Make sure you are at the correct address. Compare the name and address displayed in your app to both the package label AND the physical address on the building. Always check for the Geopin.
  2. Check the labels in a group stop. Do not assume that multiple packages in a stop are bound for the same address.
  3. Knock on the door or ring the bell between 8am and 8pm. Customers like to know their package has arrived and delivering directly helps prevent porch theft.
  4. When delivering to the customer directly, verify the name. If someone other than the customer accepts, ask for their first and last name, enter it, and get a signature.
  5. Delivering directly to the customer is the best option. When that is not available, deliver to the front door in a location protected from weather and hidden from view.
  6. Follow the customer's delivery instructions when safe and secure. If instructions ask for an unsecure location, call the customer to confirm.
  7. Never deliver to a customer's mailbox. Mailboxes are for the Postal Service only it is a federal offense.
  8. Scan delivered packages with the right reason codes. Customers receive notification of the scan type.
  9. Take Photo of Delivery (POD) for all applicable deliveries with a clear view of the package and surroundings.
  10. Mark the package with the appropriate RTS reason code if unable to complete delivery. RTS packages will not count as a DSB defect.
Exemptions
DSB Exemptions

Certain scenarios qualify for automatic DSB exemptions, meaning those deliveries will not count as defects against a driver's DSB score. Understanding these exemptions helps drivers follow the right steps to protect their metrics.

1 Scanned, Not Delivered, Not Returned

Three scenarios where lost packages are exempted: packages marked "Package is Missing" in the app, self-service return scans within the station geofence, and packages with status updates made by Driver Support.

2 Inaccurate Scan Usage with Customer Contact

DSB defects for Inaccurate Scan Usage (Unattended Delivery) or Delivered more than 50m from Geopin will be proactively exempted when the driver contacted the customer to align on an alternate location.

3 OTP Deliveries

Any package associated with a one-time password (OTP) delivery where the driver input the correct OTP is exempted from impacting DSB.

4 Attended or Simultaneous Deliveries to Business Locations with Valid Photo

Applies when: 1) The DA completed an attended delivery, 2) The DA took a valid POSD or POAD, and 3) The DA has no other DSB defects for that Tracking ID.

5 Scheduled Deliveries

Any AMXL package with a DNR concession but associated with a scheduled delivery where the driver completed the install is automatically exempted.

Scanned, Not Delivered, Not Returned: Best Practices

  • If DA does not have the package, mark it missing at the stop. If marking more than three packages as missing, contact Driver Support.
  • If DA cannot deliver the package, bring it back to the station (applies for all RTS codes).
  • At RTS, activate "Self-Service Return," scan all packages, and leave them in the appropriate RTS bin. These receive an automatic DSB exemption.
  • Verify each DA's itinerary when returning to station. If itineraries still show packages in transit, partner with OTR to submit a Shipment Scrub SIM.
  • Driver Support contact is required for the Shipment Scrub SIM to be approved.
Quality Metric
Photo on Delivery Acceptance Rate

POD Acceptance Rate measures the percentage of usable, high-quality photos taken out of all PODs submitted. Only clear, accurate photos that help customers find their packages count as accepted.

The POD photo is often the only proof customers see that their package was delivered safely. It must clearly show the package and surroundings without revealing personal information. Unclear or incomplete photos cannot be shared with customers and are rejected. A strong POD record builds customer trust and reduces "missing package" claims.

Common Rejection Reasons
  • Blurry or out-of-focus image
  • Human body parts or faces visible
  • Address labels or license plates in frame
  • Package not clearly visible or blocked
  • Photo taken from vehicle or too far away
  • Photo too dark to show package location
  • Package in hand or inside car when photo taken
Driver Success Tips
  • Take photo in good lighting. Step back and steady your camera
  • Center the package in frame with some surrounding context
  • Review each photo. Tap "Retake" if unclear (no penalty)
  • Exclude faces, reflections, and personal details
  • Show exact placement so customer can find the package easily
  • Never place packages inside USPS mailboxes (federal offense)
  • Photos help customers locate their packages. Make them count

Seatbelt Speeding Sign/Signal Distractions Following

On-Road Safety Score (OSS)

The OSS measures driver safety behaviors that are predictive of collision risk. It is composed of five behavioral metrics plus FICO, each weighted by their correlation to collisions. Event rates are normalized into 0–100 scores across Fantastic, Great, Fair, and Poor tiers.

Safety Metric
Seatbelt-Off Rate

A seatbelt-off event occurs whenever the vehicle is in motion and the DA does not have their seatbelt properly fastened including wearing the shoulder belt behind the back or the lap belt not over the hips. Events trigger once between vehicle stops.

Source Hierarchy
  • 1) Netradyne ML camera vision detects proper seatbelt wear; GPS confirms vehicle motion
  • 2) Geotab Onboard diagnostics detect seatbelt status; speedometer + GPS confirm motion. Available in all branded vehicles
  • Geotab data is suppressed if the seatbelt sensor is faulty (events exceed address stops or 100 events)
Tips for DAs
  • Position shoulder belt across the middle of your chest, away from neck
  • Place lap belt across hips, not stomach
  • NEVER put shoulder belt behind back or under arm
  • Don't place anything between chest and belt
  • Don't slide between shoulder and lap belt
  • Never use seatbelt for undesignated tasks (e.g., securing items to seat)
Safety Metric
Speeding Event Rate

A speeding event is traveling 10 mph or more over the posted speed limit for a 5-second duration, or any instance of exceeding 85 mph. In some environments, 5 seconds is roughly one city block.

Source Hierarchy
  • 1) Netradyne GPS for speed; ML camera reads speed limit signs (falls back to database)
  • 2) Geotab Vehicle speedometer for speed; database for limits. Available in all branded vehicles
  • 3) Safeguard GPS for speed; database for limits. Amazon's in-app telematics, available in ALL vehicles regardless of type
Tips for DAs
  • Always observe posted speed limits no exceptions
  • Be aware of changing speed zones (school, residential, construction)
  • Allow extra time rather than rushing to complete route
  • Remember: exceeding 85 mph triggers an event regardless of the speed limit
  • DAs exceeding speed limits can also receive Tier Infractions which may lead to offboarding
Safety Metric
Sign / Signal Violations Rate

A sign/signal violation occurs when a DA runs a stop sign without stopping (traveling at least 3 mph), executes an illegal U-turn, or runs a red light. Red-light violations are weighted 10× and severe stop-sign violations (no stop, >6 mph) are weighted 5× due to their strong correlation with collision risk.

Key Details
  • Source: Netradyne only ML camera vision detects violations; GPS confirms speed
  • Rolling stop (3–6 mph) = moderate violation still impacts OSS
  • No stop (>6 mph) = severe violation impacts OSS at 5× weight
  • Red-Major (light was red before entering intersection) impacts OSS at 10× weight
  • Red-Minor (entered on yellow, turned red) does NOT impact OSS
Tips for DAs
  • Come to a complete stop at every stop sign no rolling stops
  • Stop before the stop line or crosswalk, not in the intersection
  • Never make a U-turn unless clearly permitted by signage
  • On yellow lights, stop if safe to do so do not accelerate through
  • Icy or adverse conditions require even more stopping distance. These events are not excused by weather
Safety Metric
Distractions Rate

A distraction event is triggered when a DA looks down and away from the road for more than a few seconds (at 15+ mph), looks at their phone for more than a few seconds (at 5+ mph), or talks on their phone (at 5+ mph).

Key Details
  • Source: Netradyne only ML camera vision detects distraction; GPS confirms speed
  • Mentor-detected distractions do NOT impact this metric
  • If the internal-facing camera is obstructed, Netradyne can't detect distractions the DA won't receive a score for that day
  • Hands-free devices do NOT trigger events as long as eyes stay on the road
Tips for DAs
  • Use phone mounts set up your route BEFORE driving
  • Many events happen right after getting back in the vehicle and starting to drive. Check surroundings first
  • Use hands-free devices for calls when needed
  • Never hold or look at your phone while the vehicle is moving
  • Ensure the internal-facing camera lens is clean and unobstructed
Safety Metric
Following Distance Rate

A following distance event triggers when a DA has 0.6 seconds or less of space with the vehicle in front for a duration of 10 seconds. The 10-second window is an opportunity to correct all following distance events are considered controllable.

Key Details
  • Source: Netradyne only ML camera measures distance; GPS calculates speed and closing time
  • 4 subtypes exist (lane travel, various lane changes) all triggered by the same 0.6-second / 10-second rule
  • Both moderate and severe events impact the OSS
  • Events are NOT excused even if another car cut in front the DA has 10 seconds to create space
Tips for DAs
  • Maintain at least a 3-second following distance at all times
  • If a car cuts in front, immediately ease off the gas to create space
  • You have 10 seconds to correct before an event triggers use them
  • Increase distance in heavy traffic, rain, or poor visibility
  • Avoid tailgating even when running behind on route
